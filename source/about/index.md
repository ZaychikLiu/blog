Hi, I am Feiyang Liu.

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Automation at South China University of Technology, expected to graduate in 2027.

## Research Views

My views have shifted quite a bit over the past few months.

I used to be a committed believer in Vision-Centric intelligence: the idea that vision could remain the center of the system and approach general intelligence through visual proxy tasks and the scaling of visual representations. I now lean toward a different view: the future will come from multimodal tasks trained from scratch.

I used to be equally enthusiastic about representation learning. In the era of industrial-scale scaling, I still believe representations are fundamental, but representation-learning methods themselves are not. We should not redesign architectures merely to force representations to have some predefined target properties. Data is the best regularizer.

I also used to be skeptical of language as a modality because it carries so much human bias. My current view is that models are both denoising bias and learning bias. Large-scale models especially need to learn bias, because bias, in this sense, is knowledge; and knowledge is representation.

I also used to believe in scaling 3D Vision as a general backbone. Today, I see 3D Vision less as the backbone itself and more as a medium for control and interaction.

I once thought world models should avoid explicitly predicting pixels. I now think a world model should in fact be a family of models whose answers are pixels. Video generation, and vision models more broadly, should not fully take on the role of planner; they should focus on rendering.
